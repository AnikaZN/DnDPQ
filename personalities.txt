REFERENCE: Dungeon Master's Guide, 5E

Acting
Players who enjoy acting like getting into character and speaking in their
characters' voices. Roleplayers at heart, they enjoy social interactions with
NPCs, monsters, and their fellow party members.
- Give them opportunities to develop their characters' personalities and
backgrounds
- Allow them to interact regularly with NPCs
- Add roleplay elements to combat encounters
- Incorporate elements from their characters' backgrounds into adventures

Exploring
Players who desire exploration want to experience the wonders that a fantasy
world has to offer. They want to know what's around the next corner or hill.
They also like to find hidden clues and treasure.
- Drop clues the hint at things yet to come
- Let them find things when they take the time to explore
- Provide rich descriptions of exciting environments and use interesting maps
and props
- Give monsters secrets to uncover or cultural details to learn

Instigating
Players who like to instigate action are eager to make things happen, even if
that means taking perilous risks. They would rather rush headlong into danger
and face the consequences than face boredom.
- Allow them to affect their surroundings
- Include things in your adventures to tempt them
- Let their actions put the characters in a tight spot
- Include encounters with NPCs who are as feisty and unpredictable as they are

Fighting
Players who enjoy fantasy combat like kicking the tar out of villains and
monsters. They look for any excuse to start a fight, favoring bold action over
careful deliberation.
- Spring unexpected combat encounters on them
- Vividly describe the havoc their character wreaks with their attacks and
spells
- Include combat encounters with large numbers of weak monsters
- Interrupt social interaction and exploration with combat

Optimizing
Players who enjoy optimizing their characters' capabilities like to fine-tune
their characters for peak combat performance by gaining levels, new features,
and magic items. They welcome any opportunity to demonstrate their character's
superiority.
- Ensure steady access to new abilities and spells
- Use desired magic items as adventure hooks
- Include encounters that let their characters shine
- Provide quantifiable rewards, like experience points, for noncombat encounters

Problem Solving
Players who want to solve problems like to scrutinize NPC motivations, untangle
a villain's machinations, solve puzzles, and come up with plans.
- Include encounters that emphasize problem-solving
- Reward planning and tactics with in-game benefits
- Occasionally allow a smart plan to grant an easy win for the players
- Create NPCs with complex motives

Storytelling
Players who love storytelling want to contribute to a narrative. They like it
when their characters are heavily invested in an unfolding story, and they
enjoy encounters that are tied to and expand an overarching plot.
- Use their character's backgrounds to help shape the stories of the campaign
- Make sure an encounter advances the story in some way
- Make sure their characters' actions help steer future events
- Give NPCs ideals, bonds, and flaws that the adventurers can exploit
